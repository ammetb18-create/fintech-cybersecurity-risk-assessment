# Risk Identification

This section identifies key cybersecurity, fraud, and operational risks affecting a digital lending platform that processes sensitive customer data.

## 1. Identity Fraud

Risk:
Applicants may submit false or mismatched identity information to obtain financial services.

Description:
Inconsistent data between IDs, application forms, and supporting documents can indicate potential fraud.

Impact:
- Financial loss
- Regulatory issues
- Incorrect risk evaluation

---

## 2. Data Breach

Risk:
Unauthorized access to customer personal and financial data.

Description:
Sensitive data such as IDs, addresses, and banking information may be exposed if systems are compromised.

Impact:
- Legal penalties
- Loss of trust
- Compliance violations

---

## 3. Data Integrity Issues

Risk:
Incorrect, inconsistent, or manipulated customer data.

Description:
Errors in data validation or failure to properly verify information can lead to incorrect decisions.

Impact:
- Poor underwriting decisions
- Operational inefficiencies
- Increased fraud exposure

---

## 4. Phishing Attacks

Risk:
Employees may be targeted by phishing emails.

Description:
Attackers may gain access to internal systems by compromising employee credentials.

Impact:
- Data exposure
- System compromise

---

## 5. Insider Threat

Risk:
Internal employees misusing access to sensitive data.

Description:
Improper access controls may allow unauthorized use of customer information.

Impact:
- Data leaks
- Compliance issues

---

## 6. Weak Access Control

Risk:
Users having more access than necessary.

Description:
Lack of role-based access increases risk of data misuse.

Impact:
- Data exposure
- Internal security risks
